RAZORFISH : HPE.COM

Summer Internship, 2015

During the internship, I helped with the development of HPE.com website. The cloud team being me and two other designers, we worked on the first pass in laying out the Cloud Solutions page and its 3 child pages, during the two design sprint period I was there. We consulted BA’s and SME’s and mapped out the structure within the existing website to understand the product information. With the zone templates as reference, we created iterations of wireframes and designed new components that simplifies user experience.



THE LAYERS OF PAGES EACH HAVE SPECIFIC AUDIENCE.

The two layers of pages each have specific audience, Cloud solutions gateway page for C-level leaders to quickly grasp an idea of cloud, and the 3 children capability gateway pages for technology/engineer leaders who have deeper knowledge about the products to suggest solutions for their business based on their use case.

The end goal for the child pages were to have the technology leaders to have a better sense of what products they need according to their use case and call a representative to finalize a solution. We wanted the user to be able to see all the possible products before they makes a selection but considering language adoptability, the final version was compromised to using an accordion to fit all the information.

During my internship, I wanted to gain some production experience in visual design as well, so I talked to creatives team and helped mock up several pages in Photoshop according to HPE's style guide, and helped created the "Connect with Us" banner.



THERE WERE CHANGES TO THE INTERNAL STRUCTURE TO OPTIMIZE WORK FLOW.

It was not all production. During the more relaxing times, we helped QA log design and UX bugs for pages that are being developed and getting ready to be launched since we have a better idea of how the pages and interactions should look like. We also helped working through all 200+ pages on the sitemap to check the organization of information and consistency of components used across all pages of a same level, and rework the zone diagrams to reflect any changes.

It was the biggest project I’ve ever been part of, and same for Razorfish, so there were ongoing changes to the internal structure to optimize work flow. The creatives were working on copy and design while we work on the wireframes, so there were back and forth decisions being made, so for later sprints, we began wireframing the pages during debriefing session with PM's so the wireframes can move on to creatives. I was impressed that Razorfish saw the problem and took action to adopt a new workflow in a middle of a project.

One of the most valuable experience was I got to sit in client meetings to learn how Razorfish leaders communicate design decisions to the clients, and how to handle client feedbacks.
