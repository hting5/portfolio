REVIVE

Autumn, 2015

Humans hold onto a lot of items due to emotional attachments, making it difficult to get rid of items that aren't useful to them anymore, which causes unnecessary accumulation, and a waste of potential resources. We created Revive, an app that facilitates neighborhood-based gifting that gives away usable unwanted items to someone who needs them more in the community. You can read our complete report here.

SECONDARY RESEARCH

We looked into researches that have been done related to waste, and existing products on the market. Our biggest takeaway was what we didn't want to design. We decided to explore the space surrounding object accumulation before it becomes trash to address the root of the problem.



PRIMARY RESEARCH

We asked what social factors influence the way people age 20-25 emotionally attach to items at home? How do people differentiate personal belongings and trash? What factors contribute to whether or not a person will purchase a new product?

We conduct in-person interview with 8-12 participants at their household to observe their interactions with the environment. each interview will take approximately 60 minutes, and be done by two team members to interview and document.

MAKING SENSE OF OUR DATA

We went through four rounds of affinity diagramming to organize our data points, come up with surface insights, derive insights in the context of people, and ideate. We found that:

_1. Proximity plays a key role in second hand buying and selling
_2. Personal associations with possessions often forge a significant connection to the object, whether we like it or not.
_3. The value of an object is defined by much more than price.
_4. Some of the most prized possessions in our lives are ones that directly enable us to achieve goals.

With our insights, we came up with five design objectives as our guide to concepting to solve the waste problem. We did a few rounds of charettes and pulled together our best five ideas that reflect our design objectives and presented to our peers for critique, and decided to combine two of them to create the Revive app.

The object lifespan hits different points incorporating when a person plans to buy an object, buys it, uses it effectively, etc. The space in that cycle that Revive targets is when the user would usually decide to put an object on a shelf or in storage. This time is when an object is still practically valuable and useful, but has become static or dormant in the first user’s life. By targeting that space and making the transfer in ownership at that time, Revive enables the object to have a second life while it is still valuable. This serves emotional value for both the first and second user, and ultimately brings the object back to life.
